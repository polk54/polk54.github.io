Data Automation Lab
@ World Bank

I am the lead of the Data Automation Lab in the Development Economics Debt Statistics group at the World Bank. We are building programs that will help move the department away from manual data processing, freeing up time for increasing data quality and conducting more analysis.

The Lab serves to automate the data preparation process for the International Debt Statistics (IDS) database and annual publication. IDS provides a wealth of statistics and analysis on the external debt stocks and financial flows (debt and equity) of the world’s economies in 2017. It offers more than 200 time-series indicators from 1970 to 2017 for most reporting countries.

Every year, the Debt Statistics team compiles thousands of new financial obligations from countries and multilaterals to create International Debt Statistics. Once the data is collected from countries’ Central Banks and Ministries of Finance, the Debt Specialists spend months processing the data.

Automation offered a unique opportunity to increase efficiency and standardization in data processing. Less time and labor is spent on repetitive tasks, such as cleaning, coding, and formatting data. Methods of preparation are also becoming codified. Accordingly, the Debt Statistics team created a “Data Automation Lab.” I serve as the lead, focusing on developing reusable and modular Python code that automates these repetitive tasks.
